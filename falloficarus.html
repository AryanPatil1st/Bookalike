The Fall Of Icarus

In the ancient land of Crete, there lived a master craftsman named Daedalus, who built a labyrinth to imprison the Minotaur, a beast of legend. His skill and wisdom were unmatched, and he became the most trusted servant of King Minos.
One day, Daedalus and his son Icarus were imprisoned by the king, who feared Daedalus' ability to escape. Determined to flee, Daedalus crafted two pairs of wings out of feathers and wax for himself and Icarus. Before they took flight, Daedalus warned his son: "Do not fly too low, for the waves will dampen your wings; but do not fly too high, for the sun will melt the wax."
